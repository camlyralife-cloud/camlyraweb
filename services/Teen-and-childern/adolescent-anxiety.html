Frequently asked questions

Art therapy is beneficial for people of all ages including children, adults, and elderly individuals dealing with emotional or psychological challenges.

No artistic skill is required. Art therapy focuses on expression, not artistic quality.

It involves creating art under the guidance of a therapist to explore emotions, thoughts, and experiences.

Drawing, painting, sculpting, collage, and digital art are commonly used depending on the individual’s comfort.

What is Art Therapy?

Art Therapy is a form of psychotherapy that uses the creative process of making art to improve emotional, mental, and physical well-being.

It helps individuals express thoughts and emotions that are difficult to communicate verbally. Through guided creative activities, it supports healing, self-discovery, and emotional balance.

What It Helps With

  • Emotional Expression: Helps express hidden emotions safely.
  • Stress Reduction: Promotes relaxation and calmness.
  • Mental Health Support: Helps with anxiety, depression, PTSD.
  • Self-Esteem: Builds confidence through creativity.
  • Cognitive Growth: Improves focus and problem-solving skills.

Basic Information

  • Sessions: 45–60 minutes individual or group sessions.
  • Techniques: Drawing, painting, sculpting, collage.
  • Settings: Clinics, schools, hospitals, community centers.
  • Benefits: Emotional healing and improved well-being.
  • Who Can Join: Suitable for all age groups.
